About Me

My Story

As kids, we’re encouraged by our teachers that we can be anything we want to be when we grow up. I’m one of those kids who took that literally, and I now have quite an array of experiences under my belt. I’ve changed careers multiple times - from wedding planning, to makeup artistry, then behavioral therapy, and eventually marketing. With all of this experience, I was finally able to figure out what I want to do as an adult. Combining my knowledge of the human response with my talent for visual design and creativity, I discovered UI design. And although my background may seem like it has no common denominator, all of these experiences share one goal - my innate desire to spread happiness. Whether it was contributing to a bride’s special day, facilitating a new friendship with an autistic child, or introducing the joy of tasty Japanese food across America, I found value in helping people. As a UI designer, I’m able to spread happiness to more people on a grander scale through my user-centered design skills. And as a fully grown adult, that is exactly what I want to do.

I currently call Los Angeles, California my home, but I was born and raised in Tokyo, Japan. My favorite activities include thrilling hikes (especially trails in Hawaii) and spending hours watching cute dog videos while hoping to one day have a puppy of my own.
